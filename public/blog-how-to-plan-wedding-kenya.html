Wedding Planning
Planning Tips Updated April 2026 7 min read

Planning a wedding in Kenya can feel exciting and overwhelming at the same time. The easiest way to reduce stress is to break the process into clear steps and make your major decisions early.

1. Set Your Budget First

Your budget shapes almost every decision that follows. Before you look at venues or decor ideas, agree on an overall number and decide what matters most to you.

  • Allocate your spending across venue, tents, decor, catering, outfits, and entertainment.
  • Keep a buffer for transport, weather backup, and last-minute additions.
  • Choose two or three top priorities so you spend with intention.

2. Choose the Venue and Date

The venue affects guest comfort, logistics, and the type of setup you will need. Outdoor weddings in Nairobi, Karen, Limuru, and Naivasha all come with different tent and access considerations.

  • Pick a date early, especially during peak wedding months.
  • Confirm whether the venue already has power, parking, and washrooms.
  • Check whether the venue is suitable for tents and weather protection.

3. Build Your Guest List

Guest count directly affects tent size, seating, catering, and staffing. Keeping this number realistic will help you manage the entire budget more effectively.

4. Plan the Tent and Layout

For outdoor weddings, the tent is often the most important structure on site. Stretch tents work well for stylish premium weddings, while A-frame tents are useful when you need a more formal rectangular layout.

  • Think about guest movement from ceremony to reception.
  • Plan separate areas for dining, lounge seating, and entertainment.
  • Always include a weather backup plan.

5. Choose Decor and Theme Direction

Your decor should match both your budget and your venue. A clean, focused design usually looks stronger than trying to add too many competing details.

  • Choose a color palette early.
  • Decide where the visual focal points will be.
  • Use lighting and draping to elevate the tented space.

6. Confirm Key Suppliers Early

Once the basics are locked in, secure the suppliers that will have the biggest effect on the day: tent provider, planner, caterer, decor team, photographer, and entertainment.

7. Work With a Clear Timeline

A strong timeline keeps the planning manageable. Break the process into monthly decisions, then weekly check-ins as the date gets closer.

  • 6 to 9 months out: budget, venue, guest estimate, planner.
  • 3 to 5 months out: decor direction, tent layout, catering, outfits.
  • Final month: confirmations, weather plan, guest logistics, and final payments.

Final Thoughts

The best weddings feel smooth because the planning was clear, not because the budget was unlimited. If you get the guest flow, setup, tent plan, and supplier coordination right, the day feels effortless for everyone attending.
