Hidden Costs in Event Planning (Kenya Edition)

A guide to the costs clients often miss when planning weddings, parties, and corporate events in Kenya.

Pricing Updated April 2026 5 min read

Many event budgets look manageable at the beginning, then suddenly grow because of costs that were never discussed early enough. Knowing these hidden costs helps you budget more accurately and avoid last-minute pressure.

1. Transport and Logistics

Transport costs often change depending on venue distance, road access, and how many trips suppliers need to make. A setup in Karen is different from a setup in Naivasha or deeper rural locations.

2. Power Backup

If the venue does not have enough power for lighting, sound, catering, or technical equipment, you may need a generator. Fuel, delivery, and technical support can all add to this line item.

3. Venue Restrictions and Extra Charges

Some venues charge for extra setup time, cleaning, overtime, or security. Others limit noise levels or restrict certain types of structures.

4. Weather Backup

Outdoor events often need more than just one tent price. Flooring, side covers, drainage plans, and protected service areas can all become necessary if weather changes.

5. Setup and Breakdown Labor

Large events need more hands. If the event requires an early morning setup, a late-night breakdown, or complex decor installation, labor costs can increase quickly.

6. Permits and Public Space Requirements

If your event is happening in a public or semi-public space, you may need approvals, permits, or coordination with the venue and local authorities.

How to Protect Your Budget

  • Ask suppliers what is not included in the quote.
  • Confirm transport, labor, and weather planning early.
  • Keep a contingency amount in your budget.
  • Work with a planner or setup team that can see the full picture.

Final Thoughts

Hidden costs do not have to derail your event. They only become expensive when they are discovered too late. A detailed quote and a realistic setup plan will save both time and money.
